# Proof Ladder

```yaml
style_id: proof-ladder
display_name: Proof Ladder
inspired_by: source-agnostic trust-building sales patterns
source_safety: Uses the idea of stacking evidence, not any protected wording, celebrity identity, or endorsement.
best_for: Expensive, unfamiliar, trust-sensitive, or quality-variable products.
avoid_when: The seller cannot show proof, demonstrate quality, or support factual claims.
formula: buyer doubt -> visible proof -> comparison criteria -> low-risk next step
evidence_strategy: Search or ask for measurable proof before writing; prioritize official product specs, test reports, visible demo footage, price/value comparison, capacity, certification, guarantee, and dated availability.
evidence_needed: Product variety, origin, packing time, quality footage, guarantee, replacement policy, price basis.
durian_example: "If this is your first serious durian, do not buy on price alone. Look at the flesh: deep yellow, creamy, not watery, opened at the ripe window. We show the tray before packing so you can judge the texture before you pay. Start with one portion and compare the first bite against what cheap random stalls usually give you."
verification_test: Test whether buyers ask fewer quality questions after seeing the pitch with opening footage.
```
