# Three Angle Sales Pitch

## Quick Start

Ask for the product, audience, channel, and proof points if any are missing. Then produce three short pitches with different persuasion jobs:

1. **Proof angle** - make the buyer trust the offer.
2. **Friction angle** - name the buyer's resistance and make it feel manageable.
3. **Scene angle** - make the buyer picture a social, emotional, or status moment.

Use [references/durian-usecase.md](references/durian-usecase.md) as the calibration example.

## Workflow

1. **Pin the offer**
   - Product or service
   - Buyer segment
   - Channel and length
   - Known evidence: origin, price, guarantees, delivery, user proof, demo result

2. **Choose the three jobs**
   - Proof angle: facts, comparison, numbers, visible quality, credible source
   - Friction angle: smell, risk, price, inconvenience, learning curve, doubt, time cost
   - Scene angle: gifting, hosting, status, nostalgia, insider taste, challenge, belonging

3. **Write each pitch**
   - Hook: one sentence that names the buyer's situation
   - Body: two to four sentences that carry the angle
   - Close: one direct next step
   - Evidence needed: list claims that must be verified before publishing

4. **Verify the set**
   - The three pitches must not be paraphrases of the same idea.
   - Each pitch must contain at least one concrete buyer reason.
   - Any celebrity, authority, statistic, origin, or health claim must be marked as needing evidence unless the user supplied proof.
   - The final answer must say which pitch is strongest for the requested channel.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent endorsements. "Celebrity-style" means learning from a public speech pattern, not claiming a celebrity used the product.
- Do not make medical, health, origin, or quality claims without proof.
- Keep each script usable as spoken language; avoid abstract marketing labels inside the script.
- For luxury or taste-driven goods, sell confidence and occasion, not only features.
